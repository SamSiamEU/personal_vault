## The Hospitality Lens — How Dennis Works

The through-line in how Dennis works is that he never left the 
hospitality industry — he just changed the room he's serving in. 
The same standard applies to every team he leads.

**Questions before execution.**
Challenge before building. The question is never "what do you need 
built?" — it's "why does this problem exist, and is solving it the 
right move?" Interrogate the brief before honouring it.

**Under-promise, over-deliver — as a structural commitment.**
The commitment made to a client is the floor, not the ceiling. 
Never report something as done until it's done. Pressure-test 
numbers, accept downward revisions, never let optimism corrupt 
the forecast.

**Client-first. Not client-pleasing.**
The truth gets told even when uncomfortable. Care about actual 
outcomes more than metrics that look good in a deck. Anticipate 
what is needed before being asked.

**Mise en place as a philosophy.**
Preparation before execution. The kitchen is set before service 
begins — that's not caution, that's professionalism.

**The equation thinking.**
Ingredients and execution are inseparable. Quality must hold at 
every stage of the chain.
