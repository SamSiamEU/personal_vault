Architects leverage both to apply the right engine to the right integration pattern, optimizing for both performance and long-term scalability.

**Leverage MuleSoft and Informatica as specialized partners.** MuleSoft and Informatica are two foundational pillars of a modern integration strategy. Sophisticated enterprises require both to meet complex architectural needs. These platforms deliver maximum value when used side-by-side. This combined approach decouples application connectivity from data management, ensuring that architects choose the right platform and pattern for long-term scalability.

**MuleSoft leads on Application Connectivity patterns:**

- **Real-time API Mediation and Governance**: MuleSoft provides the foundation for an API-led connectivity approach. It manages and secures synchronous and asynchronous interactions though experience, process, and system APIs.
- **Event Handling and Orchestration**: MuleSoft consumes and publishes events across Salesforce and external queues. It orchestrates complex, multi-step business processes where control flow, latency, and transactional correctness are critical.
- **Transactional Reliability:** MuleSoft ensures data integrity within short-lived transactions (for example, creating a customer record across CRM and ERP systems) where failures require immediate rollback or specific error handling.
- **AI Agent and workflow automation:** MuleSoft Agent Fabric supports multiple pillars for the agentic enterprise–discovery, orchestration, governance, and observability–with products included in its MuleSoft Anypoint platform.

**Informatica leads on Data Management patterns.**

- **Large-scale Data Movement and Transformation**: Informatica optimizes Extract, Transform, Load (ETL) or Extract, Load, Transform (ELT) processes for massive datasets—the traditional domain of data warehousing and data lake ingestion.
- **Data Quality, Lineage, and Governance**: Informatica provides enterprise-grade capabilities for data quality and profiling. It tracks data lineage from source to consumption and enforces long-lived data governance policies across the enterprise.
- **Master Data Management (MDM)**: Informatica creates and maintains the "single source of truth" of key data entities (Customer, Product, Supplier, and others) by consolidating data from disparate systems.

**Integration Strategy**: When selecting either MuleSoft or Informatica, an enterprise must first assess the integration strategy required for the use case. They must assess if the project prioritizes data consistency or service exposure. The answer determines which platform provides the foundation of the integration capability framework.

**Platform Selection**: Architects must evaluate the primary requirement of the use case. Interactive processes composed from independent services require MuleSoft. Conversely, bulk data movement, transformation, and management require Informatica. Complex architectures may require both.

To succeed in the new agentic era, enterprises must address two critical requirements:

1. **Dynamic Connectivity:** This is where MuleSoft excels. It provides the real-time orchestration layer. It manages the stateful connection of applications, APIs, and agents to support business processes. MuleSoft enables the entire ecosystem to immediately respond to a business moment.
2. **Trusted Context (Reliable Data):** This is Informatica's strength. Informatica provides the data management layer. It executes high-scale ingestion and replication of data, while also providing the crucial trust layer–governing, mastering and ensuring the high-quality and reliability of data.

The following sections demonstrate how MuleSoft and Informatica work together across key foundational pillars–Integration, Data Management, and Agent Orchestration and Governance.

For every modern enterprise, successful operations rely on integrating applications and data planes. The application integration and data integration platforms are complementary, not interchangeable. Attempting to leverage one without the other creates operational blind spots and bottlenecks.

The shortcomings of siloed integration approaches are clear.

**1\. Application Integration Without Robust Data Governance**

Connecting applications, or app integration, allows the enterprise to move processes and transactions quickly across systems. However, if this execution layer lacks a unified, governed, and trustworthy view of the underlying data, the resulting actions, no matter how fast, will be based on fragmented, inconsistent, or obsolete information.

Organizations can orchestrate processes quickly, but without trusted data, decisions remain high-risk. Inconsistent customer interactions, inventory management, and financial reporting stem from a lack of "single source of truth," leading to inefficiencies and regulatory exposure.

MuleSoft mitigates this risk by providing out-of-the-box compliance for highly regulated industries, carrying active certifications including FedRAMP, ISMAP, HITRUST, SOC 1/2/3, and ISO 27001. However, even when the application layer communicates securely, it may still share incoherent data.

**2\. Data Integration and Management Without Agile Application Integration**

Prioritizing data integration and management platforms—especially through a powerful data warehouse or master data management (MDM) system—is essential for cleaning, standardizing, and unifying data. These platforms create a single source of truth: a reliable, holistic view of entities like customers, products, or vendors.

While the data is clean, consolidated, and trustworthy, the mechanisms to inject its derived intelligence back into live business processes and customer-facing applications are sluggish or non-existent. Without app integration, the enterprise cannot translate a single source of truth into real-time action. High-quality data sits in the background, unable to influence an in-the-moment sales decision, a personalized service interaction, or a critical supply chain adjustment. The intelligence exists, but the operational pipeline remains blocked.

MuleSoft and Informatica offer complementary agent capabilities. MuleSoft Agent Fabric serves as the unified control plane, providing end-to-end governance, orchestration, and observability. Informatica’s data agents and MCP servers plug into this fabric, delivering trusted data access and governed data actions, such as feeding MDM data or catalog metadata to the ecosystem.

Together, they allow customers to scale their investments seamlessly. To operate this integrated ecosystem at enterprise scale, MuleSoft Agent Fabric provides a systematic, four-pillar framework:

1. **Discover:** Gain complete visibility into your agent ecosystem. The Agent Registry and built-in scanners automatically synchronize and catalog every A2A agent and MCP server—including Informatica assets and external agents from Amazon Bedrock or Google Vertex—ensuring instant discovery and reuse.
2. **Orchestrate:** Coordinate agents and APIs to execute complex workflows. The Agent Broker dynamically routes tasks to the correct agent or tool, eliminating manual handoffs and transforming scattered individual agents into a reliable, coordinated workforce.
3. **Govern:** Apply secure, policy-driven control to every interaction. Trusted Agent Identity and Flex Gateway enforce compliance and access controls across three levels: end-to-end interactions, model-layer LLM requests, and execution-layer actions.
4. **Observe:** Track agent reasoning, performance, and collaboration in real time. Agent Visualizer and centralized monitoring provide complete transparency, allowing IT teams to validate decisions, detect anomalies, and optimize agent outcomes.

The integration of MuleSoft’s control plane and Informatica’s data agents provides a unified framework for governed, real-time action. This synergy powers the agentic enterprise by anchoring agile orchestration in a foundation of trusted context.
