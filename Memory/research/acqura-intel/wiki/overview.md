## Current Thesis

The paid media measurement stack is structurally broken at multiple layers — and the market knows it. GA4 can't track impressions, caps lookback at 90 days, anonymises users, and loses up to 40% of Facebook conversions post-iOS 14. Point solutions (OWOX, Ruler Analytics) are patching individual gaps, but no single tool closes the full measurement loop. The sophisticated response is measurement triangulation: MTA + MMM + incrementality testing in combination. But this framework is operationally complex and data-intensive — most marketers can't execute it without significant tooling and analytical overhead.

**Acqura's opportunity sits above the measurement layer.** The measurement problem is being solved (imperfectly, expensively) by specialist tools. The unsolved problem is: what do you *do* with the measurement? Budget decisions, channel strategy, growth execution — the intelligence layer is missing. GTI / Growth OS should be the decision surface on top of triangulated measurement data, not another measurement tool.

## Acqura Implications

- Clients will arrive with a measurement spectrum: some have nothing (broken UTM), some have partial (GA4 only), some have sophisticated (triangulation). Acqura's GTI needs to work across this spectrum.
- The measurement problem is being commoditised (Google Meridian, Meta Robyn both open-source). The intelligence-above-measurement layer is not.
- Upper-funnel spend (brand, display) is systematically undervalued by most clients due to impression attribution blindness. This is a high-value, underserved insight category for Acqura.
- Cookie deprecation and iOS 14 are structural tailwinds for first-party data solutions — Acqura should be designed first-party native.
