# Incrementality Testing

## Definition

Incrementality testing measures the *true causal* lift generated by a marketing campaign by comparing a treatment group (exposed to the campaign) against a control group (withheld from it). The difference in behaviour between groups — purchases, sign-ups, pipeline — is the incremental impact attributable to the campaign. It answers the critical question: "Would these conversions have happened anyway, without the ad?"

Considered the gold standard of marketing measurement because it establishes causation, not just correlation.

## Why It Matters for Acqura

Most attribution methods (MTA, MMM, platform-reported conversions) measure correlation — they can't prove a campaign *caused* a sale. Incrementality testing is the only method that can. This matters for Acqura because: (1) clients running brand or upper-funnel spend need incrementality to justify it; (2) the triangulation framework positions incrementality as the validation layer for all other measurement; (3) growth decisions built on correlation can be confidently wrong — Acqura's intelligence should be anchored in causal evidence where possible.

## How It Works

1. Audience is split: treatment group sees the ad; control group is withheld (ghost ads or audience holdout)
2. Both groups are tracked over the campaign period
3. Incremental lift = (conversion rate in treatment) − (conversion rate in control)
4. Statistical significance testing determines if the difference is real or noise
5. Findings can be used to validate or challenge MMM outputs for the same channel/period

## Key Use Cases

- **Facebook/Meta:** Meta's Conversion Lift product runs holdout experiments natively in Ads Manager
- **Google:** Geo Experiments test incremental lift by comparing regions with/without ads
- **Cross-channel:** Third-party tools run platform-agnostic incrementality tests

## Acqura's Angle

Incrementality testing is powerful but operationally heavy — it requires audience scale, experimental design discipline, and statistical literacy most marketing teams don't have. Acqura's Growth OS could add value here in two ways: (1) recommending when and how to run incrementality tests based on spend thresholds and channel mix; (2) interpreting incrementality results and connecting them to budget decisions. The test itself isn't the hard part — knowing what to do with the result is.

## Contradictions / Open Questions

- What is the minimum audience size / ad spend required to run a statistically valid incrementality test?
- How do you handle incrementality testing for always-on brand campaigns (no clean on/off)?
- Platform-run incrementality tests (Meta Conversion Lift) have a conflict of interest — do they produce reliable results?
- Does incrementality testing work for B2B with small, niche audiences?
