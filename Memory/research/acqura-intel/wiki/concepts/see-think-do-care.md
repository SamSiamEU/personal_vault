# See-Think-Do-Care (STDC)

## Definition

A business framework by Avinash Kaushik that organises audiences by *intent* — not demographics — into four clusters. The framework forces marketing strategy to account for all four, rather than competing solely for the small "Do" pool that everyone else also targets.

**Four intent clusters:**
- **See** — Largest Addressable Qualified Audience (LAQA) with *no* commercial intent. Qualification is behavioural (topical relevance), not age/income/education. The biggest pool, the most ignored.
- **Think** — A slightly smaller audience displaying *weak* commercial intent through their behaviour (e.g., researching options, comparing, reading reviews).
- **Do** — An audience with *strong* commercial intent, close to purchase. Currently receives ~97% of all marketing spend — brands compete over the same thin slice.
- **Care** — Extra-loyal customers: repeat buyers, renewers, long-term engagement. Currently the most under-served cluster despite being the easiest to influence and the most profitable to retain.

**LAQA principle**: the Q (qualified) is critical — it distinguishes behavioural qualification from demographic spray-and-pray. A See audience is qualified by topical interest, not by being 25–45/female/high income.

## Why It Matters for Acqura

STDC reveals a structural market failure that Acqura's GTI is positioned to fix. Most clients arrive with measurement and optimisation entirely concentrated on the Do cluster — they literally cannot see See, Think, or Care because their attribution models (last-click, GA4) only count conversion events. Acqura's intelligence layer should:

1. **Diagnose the intent gap**: show clients what percentage of their spend is Do-intent vs. upper-funnel — and what the addressable audience in See/Think looks like compared to what they're currently reaching
2. **Reframe LTV correctly**: LTV is a Care metric. Clients who track LTV inside Do-intent dashboards are misattributing retention success to acquisition campaigns
3. **Structure GTI outputs by intent stage** — a recommendation for a See-stage campaign should be judged by See metrics (reach, brand lift, follower growth), not by conversion rate

## How It Works

**Step 1 — Define LAQA per cluster.** Be specific about who qualifies behaviourally for each. Too wide = spray-and-pray; too narrow = artificial scarcity.

**Step 2 — Map content to intent.** Each cluster requires a different content type:
- See: entertain, inform, provide utility (no sales message)
- Think: educate, compare, address objections
- Do: convert — product pages, offers, reviews, checkout optimisation
- Care: support, loyalty, community, upgrade paths (not promotions)

**Step 3 — Align marketing channels to intent.** Paid social (Meta/TikTok) reaches See/Think at scale; paid search captures Do intent; email/CRM is the primary Care channel.

**Step 4 — Measure per-cluster, not globally.** Applying a single conversion-rate KPI across all four clusters judges fish by their ability to climb trees.

**Per-cluster metrics (starting framework):**

| Cluster | Primary metrics |
|---------|----------------|
| See | Unique visitors, Follower/subscriber rate, Brand lift, Share of search |
| Think | Return visits, Branded keyword search, Email subscribers, Bounce rate (inverse) |
| Do | Conversion rate, Revenue per visit, Average order value, ROAS |
| Care | Repeat purchase rate, Satisfaction/NPS, LTV, Engagement rate with owned channels |

**The trifecta requirement**: Content + Marketing + Measurement must all be in place simultaneously. Missing any leg produces a specific, diagnosable failure mode — see [[kaushik-see-think-do-care]] for the breakdown.

## Key Practitioners / Platforms

- Avinash Kaushik — creator; blog at kaushik.net; former Google Analytics Evangelist
- Framework predates most current ad platforms (2011 original, updated 2015) but the intent logic is durable

## Acqura's Angle

Most Acqura clients operate almost entirely in the Do cluster — all spend, all measurement, all optimisation focused on conversion. The STDC framework gives Acqura a way to show clients that they are:
- Over-competing in a crowded, expensive intent pool
- Under-investing in See/Think where CPMs are lower and audience is larger
- Missing Care entirely, which is where LTV is actually built

GTI output framing: every recommendation Acqura makes should be tagged to the intent cluster it affects. A budget reallocation from Do to Think is a fundamentally different recommendation than a bid optimisation within Do — and it requires different success metrics to evaluate.

**Caution**: STDC is from 2015. Some of the specific channel claims (Facebook, email) are dated. The intent-cluster logic holds; the execution layer needs updating for 2026 ad platform dynamics (Meta Advantage+, AI bidding, signal loss).

## Contradictions / Open Questions

- How does STDC map to performance max campaigns, where Google/Meta AI decides targeting automatically — does the intent cluster concept become irrelevant when the platform assigns audiences?
- The Care cluster is heavily under-theorised in the paid media context. Most Care investment is owned channel (email, CRM, loyalty). Does Acqura have a role in Care, or does GTI only cover See/Think/Do?
- STDC assumes you have content for each cluster. Many SMB clients don't. Does Acqura need a content strategy input before GTI recommendations are useful?
