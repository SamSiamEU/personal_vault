# See-Think-Do-Care: Winning Combo — Content + Marketing + Measurement

## Summary

Avinash Kaushik's extension of his See-Think-Do-Care (STDC) business framework, clarifying two strategic principles and adding a metrics layer. The core argument: brands obsess over audiences with "Do" intent (strong purchase intent) while ignoring the much larger "See" and "Think" pools — and most organisations are missing at least one of three required elements (Content, Marketing, Measurement) to execute a full STDC strategy. The framework is intentionally intent-driven rather than demographic-driven. Useful as a strategic lens for how Acqura's GTI should frame audience intelligence and measurement outputs.

## Key Takeaways

- **Four audience intent clusters**: See (no commercial intent — largest pool), Think (weak commercial intent), Do (strong commercial intent — receives 97%+ of marketing spend), Care (extra-loyal repeat buyers — most ignored segment)
- **LAQA**: Largest Addressable *Qualified* Audience. The Q is behavioural — intent signals, not age/income/demographics. See-intent audience is qualified but non-commercial; still worth reaching because the web now enables engagement with them at scale.
- **The trifecta**: Content + Marketing + Measurement must coexist. Each combination without the third produces a specific failure mode:
  - Marketing only → writing cheques your website can't cash
  - Content only → local maxima (SEO/social amplification is not enough at scale)
  - Measurement only → useless data without content to measure or marketing to optimise
  - Content + Marketing → can't know if it's working
  - Marketing + Measurement → most common failure mode; optimising spend without useful content
- **Metrics per intent stage** (starting point framework):
  - See → Unique visitors, Follower/Subscriber rate, Brand lift, Share of search
  - Think → Bounce rate, Return visits, Branded keyword search, Email subscribers
  - Do → Conversion rate, Revenue per visit, Average order value
  - Care → Return rate (repeat purchase), Satisfaction score, LTV engagement
- **LTV is a Care metric, not a Do metric** — a common misclassification that distorts reporting
