# The Complete Digital Analytics Ecosystem: How To Win Big

## Summary

Avinash Kaushik's systems model for the full digital analytics practice — from raw data to business impact. Lays out five core elements (Metrics, KPIs, Dimensions, Custom Reports, Advanced Segmentation), three categories of inputs (business priorities, competitive reality, analysts/tools), and a three-stage output progression (data pukes → custom reports → IABI). Includes a three-phase maturity timeline with realistic durations. The framing is strategic and diagnostic: useful for understanding where a client sits on the analytics maturity curve and what Acqura's GTI should deliver relative to that baseline.

## Key Takeaways

- **Five core analytics elements** (in order of maturity): Metrics → KPIs → Dimensions → Custom Reports → Advanced Segmentation. Segmentation is the separator between reporters and analysts — "all data in aggregate is crap."
- **The 10/90 rule**: for every $100 invested in analytics, $10 should go to tools, $90 to analysts/big brains. Tools don't generate insight — people do.
- **Three execution phases**:
  - Phase 1 — Data Capture (0–6 months): tool implementation, baseline metrics, KPI definition, data quality
  - Phase 2 — Data Reporting (6–12 months): data pukes → custom data pukes; leadership starts valuing data; investment in analysts unlocked
  - Phase 3 — Data Analysis (12+ months, never ends): insights → competitive intelligence → business impact computation
- **IABI output framework** — the standard analytics orgs rarely reach:
  - **I (Insight)**: what the data says and *why* (causal factors, not just "x happened")
  - **A (Action)**: specific steps the business should take (not a report, a recommendation)
  - **BI (Business Impact)**: quantified outcome of taking the action ("tripling Paid Search investment will increase revenue by $893k/week")
- **Inputs taxonomy**: Business priorities (from the highest leader who'll talk to you) + Competitive reality + New opportunities, combined with Analysts/Big Brains (top) and Tools (bottom)
- Data pukes are not failures — they're a natural phase. The red flag is when leadership keeps asking for more reports without asking questions.
