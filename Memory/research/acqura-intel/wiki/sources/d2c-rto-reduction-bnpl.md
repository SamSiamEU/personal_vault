# Reducing RTO in D2C Businesses: Challenges & Solutions (Including BNPL)

## Summary
India-focused article on Return to Origin (RTO) — the D2C problem where COD orders are refused at delivery. Covers the root causes (COD dependency, fraud, impulse buying), quantifies the problem (20–40% RTO for COD vs under 5% for prepaid), and proposes solutions including BNPL integration, OTP verification, AI-based risk scoring, and logistics improvements.

## Key Takeaways
- **India-specific problem**: COD accounts for the majority of D2C orders in India; 20–40% RTO rate for COD vs under 5% for prepaid
- **BNPL as RTO reducer**: Simpl, LazyPay, ZestMoney, Amazon Pay Later, Paytm Postpaid, Mobikwik ZIP at checkout — customers commit to later payment, reducing impulse refusal
- **AI-based RTO scoring tools**: GoKwik, Razorpay Thirdwatch, Shiprocket X — score COD orders for fraud/RTO risk before shipping
- **Partial COD**: charge ₹50–100 advance on COD orders to filter fake/non-committed orders
- **OTP verification**: customers must enter OTP before COD order is confirmed
- **Logistics partners** for faster delivery (reducing excitement fade): Delhivery, BlueDart, Ecom Express, XpressBees, Shiprocket
- **High-risk pin code blacklisting**: disable COD in geographies with chronic high RTO

## Notable Quotes / Data Points
- COD RTO rate: 20–40%
- Prepaid RTO rate: under 5%
- Double shipping cost for RTO orders (forward + return) plus packaging loss and inventory blockage
